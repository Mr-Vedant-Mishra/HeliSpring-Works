Understanding Double Torsion Springs

Double torsion springs are essential mechanical components designed to store rotational energy, offering high precision and control. These springs consist of two separate coils wound in opposite directions, which work together to provide a greater torque capacity. When a torque is applied, the spring twists and stores energy, exerting a counteracting force when it returns to its original position. The double-coil structure allows for improved strength and more balanced rotational motion, making them ideal for applications requiring high rotational force in confined spaces.

At HeliSpring Works, we specialize in manufacturing double torsion springs using premium-grade materials such as stainless steel, carbon steel, and high-strength alloys. These materials ensure long-lasting durability, resistance to fatigue, and enhanced performance under high-stress conditions. Whether for industrial use or consumer products, our double torsion springs are engineered to meet the specific torque, load, and dimensional requirements of our clients. With a focus on both standard and custom designs, we ensure that each spring performs optimally for its intended application.

Double torsion springs are widely used across various industries due to their versatility and reliability. In the automotive sector, they are commonly found in suspension systems, brake mechanisms, and latch assemblies. In consumer goods, double torsion springs are frequently used in items like window balances, clothespins, and retractable mechanisms for household appliances. They are also crucial in industrial machinery, where they help control movement and provide the necessary force in mechanical assemblies. Their ability to handle high torque in small spaces makes them indispensable in power tools, fitness equipment, and even medical devices.
