How Mild Steel Extension Springs Operate

Mild steel extension springs are key mechanical components used across many industries for their ability to store and release energy. These springs function by stretching when a force is applied, and once the force is removed, they return to their original shape, releasing the stored energy. This ability to return to their original form is crucial in applications where controlled tension or force is needed. The design of mild steel extension springs—featuring tightly wound coils and hooks at either end—makes them perfect for tasks requiring consistent force over a set range of motion. Factors such as the wire diameter, coil tension, material type, and the total number of coils determine the spring’s ability to meet performance standards.
